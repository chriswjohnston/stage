A Pattern Worth Discussing

Three Terms.
Three Mayors. Constant Turnover.

This isn't a recent problem — it's a pattern spanning three consecutive council terms. Nipissing Township has repeatedly seen its mayor's seat change hands mid-term by appointment, councillors depart, and vacancies filled by committee rather than voters. The institutional knowledge needed to see projects through has walked out the door again and again. Good ideas stall, decisions lack follow-through, and residents lose their democratic connection to the people governing them.

Name Draws. Reversed Votes. Resignations After Six Weeks. This Is How Our Council Has Operated.

In this term alone, Nipissing Township council has: filled a seat by name draw after deadlocking on who to appoint; appointed a councillor who then resigned mid-term; and when Councillor Scott lost the council vote for his seat, voted to return him anyway. Each event reveals a council struggling to manage its own continuity — let alone the township's long-term priorities.

Why Consistency Is the Foundation of Good Governance

Running a township isn't like chairing a one-time committee. It requires understanding multi-year capital plans, the history behind every budget line, relationships with provincial partners, ongoing legal matters, and the context behind hundreds of past decisions. When councillors leave mid-term, all of that walks out with them. This is how a $300,000 software implementation gets abandoned without anyone at the table asking why.

📋 Multi-Year Projects Stall

Capital projects, grant applications, and infrastructure plans span multiple years. Every new councillor restarts the learning curve — delaying decisions and increasing the risk of costly mistakes.

🤝 Relationships Take Time

Effective municipal governance depends on relationships with provincial ministries, neighbouring councils, and community organizations. These can't be rebuilt overnight every time someone leaves.

🗳️ Appointments Aren't Votes

When a seat is filled by appointment rather than election, residents lose their voice. The person at the table was chosen by four colleagues, not by the community.

⚡ Momentum Is Everything

Progress on broadband, recreation facilities, and communication improvements requires sustained attention over years. Churn kills momentum. Consistency gets things done.

The Real Issues

What Needs to Change

These aren't abstract positions. They're patterns I've observed in our township — and problems I'm committed to fixing.

01
⚠ Accountability

A $300,000 Software Failure — Without a Single Hard Question

Our township abandoned a $300,000 ERP system after three years of failed implementation, then approved a $50,000 replacement with little meaningful debate. A council with continuity and technical literacy would have caught this far earlier. I will ask those questions — every time.

02
Stability

Three Terms of Turnover — It's Time for Consistency

For three consecutive terms, our mayor's seat has changed hands mid-term. Councillors come and go. Institutional knowledge evaporates. Projects stall. It's time to elect people who are committed to staying — and to doing the work needed to make things actually happen.

03
Governance

Not Enough Questions at the Council Table

Good governance requires healthy, informed debate. When major spending decisions pass without discussion, that's not consensus — it's a gap in oversight. I'll bring a culture of respectful, evidence-based questioning to every meeting I attend.

04
Skills & Capacity

Council Needs Broader Skills for a Modern Municipality

Technology, finance, and communications are core to running a township in 2026. A council that doesn't understand these areas can't properly evaluate the decisions in front of it. I bring direct, practical experience in all three.

05
Communication

Residents Are Left in the Dark

The gap between what happens at the council table and what residents actually know is too wide. I'll advocate for consistent newsletters, active social media, and a website that genuinely reflects what's happening in our township.

06
Connectivity

Reliable Broadband for Rural Residents

High-speed internet affects property values, business viability, and quality of life. I'll actively pursue provincial and federal partnerships to close the connectivity gap in Nipissing Township.
